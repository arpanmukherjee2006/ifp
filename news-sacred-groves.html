Ethnobotanical Research

Ethnobotanical Heritage
of Jharkhand

A comprehensive survey of tribal sacred groves documenting
330 sites and 249 plant species across 24 districts

Tribal sacred groves serving as preservation plots for conservation and climate resilience across Jharkhand

Traditionally protected by religious sentiments, the vital role of Sacred Groves in Jharkhand serve as "preservation plots" for conservation and climate resilience. In global alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goals, specifically SDG 15 (Life on Land), SDG 13 (Climate Action) and SDG 11 (Sustainable Communities), the preservation of sacred groves represents an amalgamation of culture and ecosystem services.

Tribal Communities & Sacred Groves

8.6 Million Tribal Population across 32 Groups

With a substantial rural tribal population of approximately 8.6 million, representing 91% of Jharkhand's 32 tribal groups (Census 2011), sacred groves are observed in every district within Jharkhand. Individual tribes exercise ownership, maintain, and conduct religious observances within their designated sacred groves. Most of these sacred groves are being lost today due to rapid urbanization.

Research Initiative

ICFRE-Institute of Forest Productivity, Ranchi

Recognizing the ecological and cultural significance of sacred groves, the study on documentation and assessment of plant diversity of sacred groves of major tribes in Jharkhand was taken up by ICFRE-Institute of Forest Productivity, Ranchi. The project aimed to assess the plant diversity while documenting the status of sacred groves belonging to the Santhal, Munda, and Oraon tribes, locally referred to as Jaherthan, Marang Buru, and Sarna, respectively.

Survey Findings

All 24 Districts of Jharkhand

Spanning all 24 districts of Jharkhand, this study documented 330 geotagged sacred groves, primarily associated with the Santhal (172), Oraon (90), and Munda (57) communities, alongside 11 other tribal groups.

The floristic survey identified 249 plant species across 69 families, categorized into 98 herbs, 93 trees, 32 shrubs, and 24 climbers. Based on the Shannon diversity index, the sacred groves of East Singhbhum exhibited the highest levels of species richness as compared to the other surveyed regions.

Sacred Plant Species

Keystone Species of Tribal Worship

While sacred plant species vary across Jharkhand's groves, traditional belief dictates that a site must contain at least five Shorea robusta (Sal) trees, known locally as 'Sarjom'. Conservation Significance

Synergy of Culture & Environment

The assessment of Jharkhand's sacred groves reveals a profound synergy between tribal spiritualism and environmental stewardship. Presence of sacred groves across all districts of Jharkhand, highlight their role as vital repositories of both biological and cultural heritage. There is a growing global recognition of the ecosystem services provided by sacred groves and the biodiversity they sustain. This perspective underscores the necessity of preserving these sites, not just as cultural landmarks, but as critical infrastructure for biodiversity conservation.
