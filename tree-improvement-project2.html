Genetic improvement of Eucalyptus (AICRP-4)

Enhancing the productivity of Eucalyptus by identifying superior clones through multi-locational evaluation trials

More

Eucalyptus introduced to India in the late 17th century from Australia, now it is grown in nearly 4 million hectares and it serves multiple end uses, with pulpwood as its primary utility for paper industry. Rising domestic demand for paper has led to increase in pulpwood imports. To address this supply-demand gap, the project focuses on enhancing productivity of Eucalyptus through the selection of superior second-generation clones (developed by ICFRE-IFGTB) via multi-locational clonal trials.

Achievements:

  • Three clonal trials have been established using a Randomized Block Design (RBD), comprising 172 clones at Ranchi, 143 clones at Chandwa, and 183 clones at Hazaribagh.
  • Based on early growth performance of clones tested best performing clones have been selected based on predicted mean volume and BLUPs using Factor Analytical Mixed Model
