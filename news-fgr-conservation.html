Forest Genetic Resource
Conservation in Eastern India

Conserving 100 species across Jharkhand, Bihar, and West Bengal
through holistic documentation and propagation approaches

Conservation efforts targeting 100 species across Jharkhand, Bihar, and West Bengal

The sustainability of forest genetic resources are getting hampered due to various anthropogenic pressure and diversion of forest lands for variety of causes, therefore conservation of these forest genetic resources needs to be addressed for its availability for present and future generations. ICFRE-Institute of Forest Productivity, Ranchi initiated to conserve the important forest genetic resource targeting 100 species of eastern India, employing holistic approaches of documentation, distribution, characterization, propagation and establish their in situ conservation plots under different components. As forest trees are normally the keystone species of forest ecosystems, their continued existence is essential for many floral and faunal associations of these ecosystems.

Regional Survey & Documentation

Jharkhand, Bihar & West Bengal

The major forest divisions of three states of eastern India viz, Jharkhand, Bihar and West Bengal were surveyed and prioritized species were documented, mapped and genetic worth of prime species viz, Shorea robusta (Sal) and Buchnannia cochinchinensis (Chironjee) were characterized employing molecular markers.

Propagation & Conservation Techniques

Nursery techniques have been developed for propagation of six RET species (Pterocarpus marsupium, Buchanania cochinchinensis, Haldina cordifolia, Sterculia urens, Boswellia serrata, and Litsea glutinosa) for mass propagation and conservation.

In vitro protocol for developing slow growth culture of 8 RET FGR species (Embelia tsjerium-cottam, Embelia ribes, Vitex peduncularis, Rauwolfia serpentina, Tinospora cordifolia, Litsea glutinosa, Pterocarpus marsupium and Oroxylum indicum) has been successfully established.

Seed Storage & Management

Walk-in Seed Storage Facility, IFP Ranchi

Seeds of 42 targeted FGR species were collected from different forest divisions of three states and seed lots were collected from at least 5 provenances for each species and stored in walk-in-seed-storage facility at IFP Ranchi. The aim of genetic resources management is to improve conditions for the continuous evolution of the species, which represents the defense mechanism of organisms in suppression the environmental changes.
